Lesson: Coordinate Planes & Linear Equations

The Coordinate Plane

A coordinate plane is formed by a horizontal number line (the x-axis) and a vertical number line (the y-axis). They intersect at the center, called the origin (0, 0).

Every point on this plane has two coordinates: (x, y). The x-coordinate tells you how far left or right to go, and the y-coordinate tells you how far up or down.

Slope (Rise over Run)

The slope of a line describes how steep it is. It's often written as the fraction Rise / Run.

  • Rise: The vertical change (up or down).
  • Run: The horizontal change (always to the right).

Example: A slope of 2/3 means you go UP 2 units, and RIGHT 3 units to find the next point.

Slope-Intercept Form

y = mx + b

This is the easiest form to graph.

  • b (y-intercept): Where the line crosses the y-axis. Graph this point first! (0, b)
  • m (slope): Use the rise and run starting from the y-intercept to find the second point.

Standard Form

Ax + By = C

To graph this form, it's usually easiest to find the intercepts.

  • Find y-intercept: Plug in x = 0 and solve for y.
  • Find slope: The slope is equal to -A / B. Use this slope from the y-intercept to find the second point.
